About

Aayushi
Rajsinha

Painter, Artist & Visual Storyteller based in India. Bridging cultural traditions and bold contemporary expression through the language of color.

— Aayushi

Every stroke is a decision. Every color, a feeling.

I'm Aayushi, and I paint because silence sometimes isn't enough. There are emotions too layered for words, stories too textured for sentences — and that's where the brush finds its voice.

My journey as a painter began with curiosity — a fascination with how color could carry the weight of culture, memory, and myth. Growing up surrounded by the rich visual vocabulary of Indian art, I found myself drawn to the characters, patterns, and spiritual narratives that define our heritage.

"I don't paint what I see. I paint what I feel when I close my eyes and let the canvas breathe."

Over time, this evolved into a language of my own. Brush It Off was born from this — an invitation to experience art that is unapologetically expressive. My work spans canvas paintings, intricate mandala dot-art, bold folk figures, and modern cubist compositions — always anchored in authenticity.

Each piece begins with a feeling: a melody overheard, a moment of stillness, a burst of cultural pride. That seed grows into a sketch, then a palette, then a fully realized world on canvas. I believe art should make you feel something before you understand it.
